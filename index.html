Agent financial infrastructure · XRP Ledger

The trust bureau for AI agent payments.

MeshCredit runs Know-Your-Agent and Know-Your-Business checks, then issues the verdict as a tamper-evident credential on the XRP Ledger. Any bank reads it straight from the ledger — and enforces it at consensus: an uncertified agent's payment is rejected by the validators themselves. The product is the credential; cross-border agent payments are use case #1.

The idea

Trust that lives on the ledger, not in a database

Everyone agrees agents need identity and reputation. The real question is where it is enforced. MeshCredit pushes enforcement into the ledger itself: the credential and the payment gate are the same primitive, so the rule cannot be skipped by a buggy or malicious integrator.

Typical agent-trust stack

Enforced a layer up

Trust is a row in a vendor's database
Checked by an API call the relying party must trust
Enforced in application code, above the money
Revocation = a DB flag every integrator must honor

Enforced at consensus

Use case #1 · cross-border payment

A Swiss company's agent pays a supplier in Lagos

A KYB-verified operator's AI agent must pay a supplier abroad through a bank that gates its settlement account on a MeshCredit credential. A trustworthy agent is fast-tracked; an uncertified one is declined by the ledger before the bank ever sees it.

The trust passport & how it's scored

The verdict is minted as an XLS-70 credential — a tier, a max single-payment, an expiry, and content-attestation hashes, readable by anyone from any transaction. The 100-point score is transparent: every point is legible and defensible.
